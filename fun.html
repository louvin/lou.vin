Outdoors

Berks County Heritage Center

See where Devin worked during high school! Wide gravel trails along the Tulpehocken River connect to other county parks and historic sites. A museum with tours of some historic sites take place at the Heritage Center. Large open fields are great for frisbee golf, picnics or other activities. Learn more on the Heritage Center website.

Blue Marsh Lake

Blue Marsh is a large, man-made lake in Berks County with more than 30 miles of trails. The lake is fun for hiking, picnics, and water sports. Learn more about Blue Marsh Lake here.

Hawk Mountain Sanctuary

Hawk Mountain Sanctuary offers multiple trails across the mountain with beautiful views of the valley below and raptors above. Cultural

GoggleWorks Center for the Arts

Once a goggle factory, the GoggleWorks is now a gallery and arts workshop open to the public. Admission is free. You'll see art exhibitions using diverse media, as well as in-progress art in the studios of the artists-in-residence. Check out the opening hours and galleries on the GoggleWorks website.

Reading Public Museum

The Reading Public Museum is a "hands-on" museum for all ages. The museum features a number of permanent galleries (PA German history, Fine Arts, Ancient Civilizations, Arms and Armor), as well as rotating, interactive exhibits. The museum also features an arboretum and planetarium on-site. See the museum website for hours and rotating exhibit information.

Amish Country

Southwest of Reading is Lancaster County, the oldest Amish settlement in the US. Check out handmade crafts and furniture, learn about Amish food and culture, and maybe even take a ride in a horse-and-buggy! See the Discover Lancaster website for activities and directions.

Devin's Ice Cream Picks

The Creamery at Plum Creek Farm

Located just down the road from the venue, The Creamery serves their own soft-serve and hard ice cream, as well as other PA Dutch delights. Plum Creek Farm also runs a country store offering local food. Learn more at the Plum Creek Farm website.

Way-Har Farms

Way-Har Farms serves 40 flavors of ice cream from a local dairy (sometimes featuring pictures of the cows responsible for your ice cream!). Way-Har also features baked goods and local food in a quaint country store. See the Way-Har Farms website for details.
